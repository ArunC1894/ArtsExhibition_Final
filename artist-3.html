Sir John Lavery




MJohn Lavery was born in inner North Belfast and moved to Scotland as a child, where he attended Haldane Academy in Glasgow.
In 1878, he established his own studio which was destroyed in a fire the following year.
With the £300 insurance payout, he studied at Heatherley's School in London for a year.
Lavery then went to Paris to study at the Académie Julian in the early 1880s before returning to Glasgow
where he was associated with the Glasgow School.
In 1888, Lavery was commissioned to paint the state visit of Queen Victoria to the Glasgow International Exhibition,
which launched his career as a society painter. He moved to London soon after and became a successful portrait artist.
Overall, John Lavery was a prominent artist of his time, and his association with influential patrons such as William Burrell
helped to further his career as a society painter. His talent as a portrait artist was widely recognized,
and his portrait of Mary Burrell is a notable example of his work.
